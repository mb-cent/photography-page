Exposure Triangle

  1. ISO

    2. ISO is a unit that was created for camera film to measure how sensitive it is to light exposure.
    Higher ISO values are more sensitive to light, but also appear more "grainy" in the final result.
    Digital photography allows you to change the ISO of the digital sensor, which is one of the advantages of digital photography.

  2. Aperature Size

    3. Aperatures allow you to change the amount of light that enters the camera, which affects the depth of field of the image produced.
    Larger aperatures let more light in while reducing the depth of field of the image, while smaller aperatures reduce the amount of light whlle increasing the depth of field.
    Because depth of field has the most drastic impact on the appearance of the photo taken, it's arguably more important than ISO, especially for digital photgraphy.

  3. Shutter Speed

    4. Shutter speed is the amount of time the shutter stays open, allowing the film/sensor to take in more light.
    Faster shutter speeds are great for taking pictures of moving subjects,
    while slower shutter speeds are great for taking photos of stationary subjects, like landscapes.

Types of Lenses

  • Standard (35mm-50mm)

    • 35mm-50mm is around the focal range of our eyes, so photos of subjects taken with standard lenses look familiar.
    Frequent photography types with standard lens include portrait photography and street photography.

  • Wide Angle (<35mm)

    • Lenses less than 35mm can take in light from a wider field of view, making this ideal for landscape photography.

  • Telephoto (>80mm)

    • Telephoto lenses can focus on subjects very far away from the camera.This is mostly used for wildlife photography.
